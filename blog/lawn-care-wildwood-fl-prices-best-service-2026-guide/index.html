Do Florida Lawns Need Watering Once a Week?

Lawn watering is the scheduled application of irrigation to maintain turf health and root depth.

No — most Florida lawns need watering 2 times per week, applying 0.5 to 0.75 inches each time, not once.

The University of Florida IFAS Extension recommends two weekly irrigations during active growth (April–October) and one weekly during cooler months. Sumter County falls under the Southwest Florida Water Management District's watering restrictions, which limit irrigation to specific days based on address (source: SWFWMD). Experts at Tom's Business recommend installing a rain sensor to comply with Florida Statute 373.62 (state law requiring rain sensors on automatic irrigation systems).

What's the Difference Between Lawn Care and Lawn Maintenance?

Lawn care and lawn maintenance are related but distinct service categories with different scopes and skill requirements.

Lawn care covers fertilization, weed control, and pest management; lawn maintenance covers mowing, edging, and trimming.

Lawn care vs lawn maintenance: lawn care is treatment-focused because it addresses soil chemistry, nutrients, and pests. Lawn maintenance is appearance-focused because it manages weekly growth and curb appeal. Many Wildwood providers, including Tom's Business, bundle both into a single monthly plan.

What Credentials Should a Lawn Care Company Have?

Florida lawn care companies should hold a state pesticide license, $300,000+ general liability insurance, and ideally a Best Management Practices certification.

What to Verify Before Hiring

    • Limited Commercial Landscape Maintenance License — issued by FDACS for fertilizer and pesticide applications.

    • General liability insurance — minimum $300,000 recommended; ask for a Certificate of Insurance.

    • Green Industries Best Management Practices (GI-BMP) certification — required statewide for fertilizer applicators (per Florida Statute 482.1562).

    • Workers' compensation — protects you if a crew member is injured on your property.

Hiring Checklist for Wildwood Lawn Service

    • Confirm a current FDACS pesticide license number.

    • Request a Certificate of Insurance listing your address.

    • Get a written service agreement listing frequency and scope.

    • Verify GI-BMP certification for any fertilization work.

    • Ask which turf species they specialize in (St. Augustine vs Bahia).

    • Read reviews on Google for company names like "Gator Lawn Service" or "Oxford Lawn" before signing.

    • Confirm rain-day rescheduling and holiday policies.
